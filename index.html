Logo Rules

✓ Always

  • Use approved vector files (.svg, .eps, .ai) for all applications
  • Maintain clear space equal to the cap-height of the 'f' on all four sides
  • Use teal version as the default on white/light backgrounds
  • Include "Technology" sub-label in formal and legal contexts
  • Minimum size: 80px wide digital / 20mm wide print

✗ Never

  • Stretch, condense, rotate, or skew the wordmark
  • Recreate in a different typeface — even Gotham
  • Apply drop shadows, outlines, or effects
  • Place on busy photographic or patterned backgrounds
  • Use any colour outside the approved four logo colours

Colour Usage Hierarchy

  • Teal (#00ABAB) is the primary brand anchor — logo default, links, active states, section accents, infographic highlights.
  • Magenta (#9F007E) is the energy colour — CTAs, campaign moments, pull quotes, rally cry. Use with intent. Overuse kills its impact.
  • Navy (#19223F) is the authority — primary heading colour, dark backgrounds, navigation, formal collateral.
  • Never combine teal + magenta at equal visual weight. One leads, one supports.
  • Accessibility minimum: 4.5:1 contrast ratio for all body text (WCAG AA). Test every colour combination before publishing.

07 · Visual Identity

Imagery & Photography

People — Confident & Real

Natural moments of diverse professionals working with technology. Warm, authentic, modern environments. People look capable and at ease — never posed, never stock-photo stiff.

Security — Bold & Compelling

Strong cybersecurity visuals are approved — shields, network nodes, glowing circuits. Use dark, high-contrast imagery with blue/purple tones for security-specific content. Conveys strength, not fear.

Technology — Clean & Modern

Contemporary workplaces, cloud visualisations, connectivity imagery. Avoid blinking server room clichés. Prefer imagery showing technology enabling people, not replacing them.

Brand Motif: The hexagonal node-and-connector network pattern (visible in current collateral) is Fuse's approved background graphic. Use in cool blue-grey tones at 8–15% opacity on section backgrounds and presentation covers. Never compete with content.

Image Treatment Rules

  • High contrast, clean, modern — not vintage, muted, faded, or heavily filtered
  • Dark overlay (navy at 45–60% opacity) required when text sits on photography
  • Aerial city/technology imagery: hero sections and covers only — not body content
  • Never depict frustration, failure, or crisis — our brand communicates confidence and resolution
  • People's faces must not be cropped; always allow breathing room at image edges
08 · Visual Identity

Layout & Design Principles

Whitespace

Generous whitespace signals premium and confidence. Never crowd elements — edit the content, not the margins. Minimum 48px page margins on desktop.

Grid System

12-column grid for web. Three-column max for card layouts. 24px gutters minimum. All content aligned to the grid — no floating elements.

Hierarchy

One primary action per section. Size, weight, and colour guide attention. The most important element must be visually dominant — always.

Iconography

Consistent 2px stroke-weight line icons (Lucide or Phosphor approved). Icons supplement copy — never replace key messages. Size: 20–24px inline, 40–48px feature icons.

Copy Rules

  • Lead with the outcome, not the feature. "Your team stays protected, always" beats "Advanced threat detection enabled."
  • Active voice always. "We manage your IT" not "Your IT is managed by us."
  • Sentence case for headings. "Security built in" not "Security Built In."
  • Short sentences. Short paragraphs. Two to three sentences max in marketing copy blocks.
  • Australian English spelling: organisation, colour, recognise, licence (noun), programme, catalogue.
  • Numbers: Spell out one–nine; numerals for 10+. Always numerals for stats (3x faster, 27 countries).
  • No jargon in external copy unless audience is clearly technical. Abbreviate only after first-use definition.

Digital Applications

Website

  • Primary CTA: Magenta (#9F007E) fill, white Gotham Bold text, border-radius 6px. Hover: darken 10%.
  • Secondary CTA: Navy fill, or white with navy 2px border.
  • Body links: Teal (#00ABAB), underline on hover.
  • Section backgrounds alternate: White → Light Gray → Teal Pale or Magenta Pale for featured sections.
  • Navigation: White background, navy text, teal active state underline, magenta CTA button top-right.
  • Hero sections: Navy or dark overlay, white Gotham Bold type, hexagonal motif at 10% opacity.

Email

  • Max width 600px. White body background. Navy header strip with white Fuse wordmark left-aligned.
  • CTA button: Magenta, minimum 44px height, border-radius 6px.
  • Footer: Light gray background, mid-gray text, teal links. Include unsubscribe, ABN, and address.
  • Use Montserrat (Google Fonts) as the web-safe Gotham substitute for email clients.
  • Subject lines: max 50 characters. No ALL CAPS. No clickbait. Lead with the value or insight.

Presentations

  • Cover: Navy full-bleed, white Gotham Bold title, teal section label, hexagonal motif at 12% opacity, Fuse logo bottom-right.
  • Section dividers: Teal or magenta full-bleed with white heading — alternate to create visual rhythm.
  • Content slides: White background, navy H2 headings, charcoal body text, teal accent colour for key stats.
  • Three-column layout is approved for feature/benefit slides (matching pillar deck format).
  • Footer on every slide: Fuse logo left, slide number right. "Classified as Commercial in Confidence" on sensitive decks.
  • Close all external proposals and presentations with the rally cry: WE MANAGE YOUR IT LIKE WE OWN IT.

15 · Application

Do's & Don'ts

A fast-reference guide for anyone creating Fuse content — whether you're an internal team member, a designer, or an agency partner.

✓ Always do this
  • Use the correct teal (#00ABAB) and magenta (#9F007E) — not approximate colours
  • Anchor every piece of content to at least one messaging pillar
  • Lead with the client's outcome or benefit, not our feature
  • Use Gotham Bold for headings, Gotham Book for body
  • Reference concrete proof: ISO cert, Essential Eight, Cyber Guarantee
  • Maintain generous whitespace — less is always more
  • Use Australian English spelling throughout
  • Close proposals and presentations with the rally cry
  • Tag all campaign links with UTM parameters
  • Get marketing sign-off before publishing external content
✗ Never do this
  • Recreate or approximate the logo in any typeface
  • Stretch, rotate, recolour, or add effects to the wordmark
  • Use buzzwords: leverage, synergy, holistic, solutions-oriented
  • Mix more than 3 brand colours in a single design
  • Use teal and magenta at equal visual weight in the same element
  • Make promises we can't back with evidence
  • Post on social without reviewing against voice & tone guidelines
  • Use imagery that conveys frustration, failure, or crisis
  • Paraphrase or shorten the rally cry — use it verbatim or not at all
  • Publish competitor comparisons or unverified statistics

The brand test: Before publishing anything — ask "Does this look, sound, and feel like a company that manages IT like they own it?" If yes — you're on brand.
